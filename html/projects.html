ResilienceAI

ResilienceAI is a cutting-edge startup group that I am proud to be part of for my senior capstone project. Our organization is dedicated to delivering innovative solutions to state and local governments to enhance community resilience, allocate resources more efficiently, and mitigate the impacts of climate change. Using machine learning and big data, we design web-based software, data visualizations, and mapping applications to identify areas that are susceptible to natural disasters and ensure equitable access to disaster resources. My contributions to the project include creating a low-fidelity prototype in HTML, designing wireframes to facilitate development, conducting empathy interviews to refine our understanding of user needs, and ensuring that the MVP's UI/UX is both coherent and visually professional.

Robotic Duck Dance

As part of a three-member team, we developed a robotic simulation named "A Day in the Park," utilizing Webots and implemented using Python and C coding languages. The simulation includes diverse robot types enjoying a day at the park, designed as an exclusive human-free environment. Specifically, the TIAGo steel "moms" navigate around the pond through the shortest path, while turtle bot "ducks" swim in groups. Additionally, E-puck "kids" are found in the playground area, avoiding obstacles while running around. Our project resulted in an engaging and interactive simulation showcasing our proficiency in programming and robotic design.

Reggie!

Reggie! is a user-centered phone application designed to assist college students in managing their time effectively. Developed by a team of six, our prototype incorporates various aspects of User-Centered Design (UCD) principles. We began with a discovery research phase, followed by the development of personas and scenarios, to gain key insights into our target audience. This led us to create current state Journey Maps and future state storyboards, which served as the basis for creating information architectures (IAs) and workflows. We used this to develop a Figma model, which our team used for the app's development. The end result is a convenient and intuitive application that addresses the unique challenges of college life.
